Thirteen coffins？

According to legend, on a certain day in Nanjing hundreds of years ago, after the death of a certain great man, thirteen coffins were carried out of the thirteen city gates. After getting out of the city gate, these coffins were carried in different directions...Some historians believe that this is to prevent tomb robbers from robbery, and some historians believe that this is to hide the location of the real tomb.

So, who is the man? And why is there such a legend passed down?

Yuanzhang Zhu

1328-1398

The founder of the Ming Dynasty which lasted for 276 years. During his reign, Nanjing was the capital of the country. 

  • Born in poverty.Yuanzhang Zhu was born in a very poor peasant family. When he was a child, his father died. When the famine came, most family were starved to death. Yuanzhang Zhang was forced to become a beggar. 
  • Honored in troubled times. Yuanzhang Zhu became famous during wars because of  his courage and talent. More and more people chose to pledged loyalty to him.
  • Industrious but arbitrary as an emperor. As an emperor, Yuanzhang Zhu always maintained his grip on power. It's hard for him to trust most ministers and in order to enforce rule, Yuanzhang Zhu never resist killing. However, despite his rude attitude towards ministers, he always put the interests of the people first.

Although Yuanzhang Zhu was such a legendary emperor, 
he had to face death eventually.

Especially after the death of his beloved queen,
Yuanzhang Zhu could not help considering his own death……

Timeline

 

 

In ancient China, people believed in the world after death. The emperor, like everyone else, paid special attention to his tomb. Like many emperors, soon after Yuanzhang Zhu became the emperor, he started to think about the tomb after his death. 

 

 

According to the legend, in order to find a satisfactory location, Yuanzhang Zhu ordered three clever ministers to come up with plan. And all of them chose Zijin Mountain.

 

 

To construct this tomb, Yuanzhang Zhu recruited 100,000 soldiers and workers and this program lasted for 25 years. 

When naming this mausoleum, because the queen's posthumous name is Xiao, so this mausoleum was named Xiao.

Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

"Ming"（明）: name of the dynasty
"Xiao"（孝）: posthumous name of the queen, which means a virtue of respect for one's father, elders, and ancestors
“Ling”（陵）: also means mausoleum

Green Trees

Environment 

Apart from constructions in Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, Plants here are also remarkable. There are many old trees in the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum. As the Chinese advocate the harmony between human beings and nature, there are various ancient trees in Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum.

In ancient times, the more trees around the tomb, the higher the status of the dead. These trees also indicate the royal majesty.

Constructions

 

Minglou

 

This building is a typical Chinese style with upturned eaves and bright red and yellow color scheme. Behind Minglou is Yuanzhang Zhu and his queen's tomb.

Stone Carving

 

The stone carving on the bridge is the totem of China, dragon. In ancient dynasties, only the emperor could use the dragon pattern.

Stone Road

 

This road has been preferred by many people. On both sides of the road, there are six kinds of stone beasts, embodying the etiquette requirements of the royal.

The scenery of those roads around the Ming Xiaoling mausoleum is also very beautiful
